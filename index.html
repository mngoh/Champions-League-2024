Key Insights

📈

Goals Trend Upward Over Time

Total UCL goals have risen from 32.6 in 2008–09 to over 43 by 2022–23, reflecting more attacking play and expansion of the competition format.

🎯

XGBoost Outperforms Linear Models

Tree-based models (XGBoost, Random Forest) capture non-linear interactions between features better than linear approaches, cutting RMSE by ~14%.

⚠️

Generalization Gap Detected

RMSE increases from 0.647 (training) to 0.854 on unseen 2023–24 data, suggesting the model could benefit from richer features (xG, progressive passes, etc.).

🏆

Manchester City Dominated 2023/24

Man City scored 28 goals — the highest in the 2023–24 season. Real Madrid (24) and Bayern Munich (21) followed, consistent with their historical dominance.

🔑

Matches Played Drives Goals

MP (matches played) is the strongest predictor — teams that advance deeper naturally score more. PKatt and CrdY add disciplinary and set-piece context.

🔄

Square Root Transform Improved Fit

The target variable (goals) was right-skewed. Applying a square root transformation normalized the distribution and meaningfully improved model accuracy.
